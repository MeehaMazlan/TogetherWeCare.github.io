TYPES OF DISABILITIES

A disability is defined as a condition or function that is severely impaired in comparison to an individual's or group's customary standard. Individual functioning is referred to by the word, which includes physical impairment, sensory impairment, cognitive impairment, intellectual impairment, mental illness, and many types of chronic disease. Disability is viewed as a multifaceted experience for the individual concerned. There may be impacts on organs or body components, as well as on a person's engagement in other aspects of life.

There are numerous reasons of this type of sickness. It can be acquired by anyone through accident, injury, disease, post-surgery complications, or inheritance.     Mentally Disabled

    Any person who is diagnosed with a psychiatric disorder and receives disability benefits from the federal or state government, or who is deemed to have a comparable long-term mentally disabling condition by a qualified professional, such as a licenced psychiatrist, psychologist, or clinical social worker, who attests that the person is unable to perform life roles in at least one of the major domains of living, working, or learning, is considered mentally disabled.
